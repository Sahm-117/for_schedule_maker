FOF IKD Ops

From WhatsApp screenshots to a real product. Built with AI in weeks, not months.

Programme Scheduling PWA AI-Assisted Build

A few months ago, our church programme team was coordinating a full 8-week discipleship schedule through WhatsApp screenshots and a shared spreadsheet that nobody trusted.

Last-minute changes meant someone had to update the spreadsheet, screenshot it, and blast it to 30+ people in a chat — hoping they'd all see it before the next session. Spoiler: they didn't always.

So I did what product managers do — I asked what if there was a better way?

What I built: FOF IKD Ops — a mobile-first web app where the programme team can view the live schedule, get push notifications when something changes, and submit edits through a proper approval workflow. Installable on any phone, works offline.

Here's the part that surprises people:

I don't have a traditional software background. I'm a Product Manager. What changed is that I leaned into AI as a genuine collaborator, not just a search engine.

  • 🧠I described the problem — the schedule chaos, the specific roles and permissions needed, the "someone is always out of the loop" frustration — and we worked through the solution design together.
  • 💬When something broke (and things broke), I didn't just paste the error and wait. I explained what I expected to happen vs. what was happening. That context made all the difference.
  • 🚀When I hit a wall with a database permissions issue that was breaking everything, we diagnosed it together in minutes. It would have taken me days alone.

The result? A product that the team actually uses. Push notifications go out when schedules change. The team lead approves edits before they go live. Everything lives in one place, accessible from any phone.

What I learned about working with AI:

  • ✅The more context you give, the better the output. Vague prompts → vague solutions.
  • ✅You still need to own the decisions. AI is a very capable collaborator, not a replacement for judgment.
  • ✅It levels the playing field. I shipped something I couldn't have built in a year on my own — in weeks.

This isn't about replacing anyone. It's about expanding what's possible when you have a clear problem and the willingness to iterate fast.
